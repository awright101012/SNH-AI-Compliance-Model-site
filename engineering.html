From Rulebook to Compliance Engine

How evidence, company policy, bounded reasoning, verification, and human review become one auditable decision path.

SNH Strategic InitiativesA Wright OriginalResearch brief · July 2026

The model reasons through the case. The architecture decides whether that reasoning may become an operational decision.

The worked example shows the handoff once. This article explains how the handoff remains reliable when the record is incomplete, the guide changes, or the model produces an answer the evidence does not support.

1. Convert the guide into a decision contract

A company guide is written for trained people. It can rely on context, cross-references, exceptions, and terms whose meaning changes by branch. A model cannot be expected to infer that operating structure safely from a document dump.

The first engineering job is to make the guide testable without replacing it. The decision contract names:

  • the order in which questions must be answered;
  • the evidence required for each branch;
  • the most-specific instruction when several appear relevant;
  • the conditions that end the analysis;
  • the conditions that require review; and
  • the version of the guide that authorized the result.

Policy changes land in that source first. The model, evaluation set, and fine-tuned behavior follow. Policy does not remain hidden inside an old prompt or response template.

2. Normalize evidence without repairing it by assumption

Court and vendor records describe the same concepts in different shapes. Dispositions arrive as codes, phrases, or free text. Dates can be absent or disagree. One search can contain several courts and cases.

The normalization boundary converts those inputs into stable case facts while preserving missingness and source warnings. Rules, models, reviewers, and evaluators then see the same jurisdiction, charge, disposition, timing, identity evidence, relief indicators, and unresolved issues.

This boundary also protects the learning program. Raw names, historical people, and row-level case text remain local evidence. Model-facing training uses synthetic, masked, or normalized representations.

The adapter standardizes what arrived. It does not invent what is missing.

3. Apply policy in procedural order

Compliance decisions are sequences, not keyword matches. Identity can control before reportability. Confirmed relief can end the analysis before timing. An offense exclusion can make a later disposition branch irrelevant. Convictions, pending matters, and non-convictions can use different clocks.

The engine therefore walks the guide in a fixed order:

establish identity sufficiency
→ check record relief
→ apply offense exclusions
→ classify disposition and timing
→ decide, or preserve the exact reason review is required

Exact branches remain deterministic because they should behave identically every time. The SLM is reserved for the parts that genuinely require interpretation.

4. Bound the SLM before asking it to reason

The SLM with a harness works open-book. It receives one normalized case, the relevant portions of the current guide, and a fixed decision contract. It does not receive the entire policy universe, search the internet, or rely on legal rules remembered from pretraining.

The model’s job is narrow: connect the supplied facts to the supplied instructions and produce a structured explanation. The guide remains the authority.

This separation makes the reasoner replaceable. A hosted model, a local open-source model, or a company-specific fine-tuned SLM can occupy the same reasoning role without rewriting policy selection, verification, or the decision trace.

5. Treat the answer as an untrusted draft

Fluent reasoning is not permission. Before a response becomes an operational decision, an independent verifier checks whether:

  • the required fields are present;
  • the final answer and explanation agree;
  • every citation belongs to the current guide and the selected case packet;
  • unresolved evidence has not been ignored;
  • the policy sequence remains intact; and
  • the answer can be connected to the guide version used at decision time.

The verifier proves contract compliance and provenance. It does not claim to be an independent legal oracle.

If the contract fails, the harness rewrites the operational outcome to manual review while retaining the attempted answer and the reason it was blocked.

An unsupported answer may remain visible as evidence of what the model tried. It may not remain executable as a final compliance decision.

6. Decide every case before aggregating the search

A screening search can contain several cases and charges. A primary-record shortcut can miss a reportable sibling or hide a case that still requires review.

The engine adjudicates every case separately and preserves its rationale. The search-level rollup happens last: a reportable case controls; otherwise review controls; the search is exempt only when every case supports that result.

This is a software boundary with an operating consequence. Reviewers can see which case controlled the search and which cases still need attention instead of receiving one unexplained label.

7. Preserve a trace that supports review and learning

Every permitted decision carries a reconstruction record: normalized facts, guide version, selected instructions, reasoning output, verifier findings, and final disposition.

That trace answers different questions for different owners:

  • Operations can see why the case was decided or routed.
  • Compliance can see which company instruction controlled.
  • Engineering can isolate whether a failure began in evidence, policy mapping, reasoning, or verification.
  • Model training can learn from approved synthetic traces without copying historical case rows.

The outcome can improve the evaluation set, clarify the guide, or motivate a new safeguard. It cannot silently rewrite the company’s policy.

How a second company becomes a second engine

The reusable asset is the conversion process, not one customer’s answer set.

  1. Define authority. Name the company guide, counsel-approved interpretations, customer overlays, and review policy.
  2. Make the guide testable. Encode precedence, exceptions, evidence requirements, and abstention conditions.
  3. Normalize the record. Build stable case facts while preserving missingness and warnings.
  4. Run the governed engine. Evaluate the SLM beside human reviewers with every handoff visible.
  5. Generate synthetic cases. Cover ordinary decisions, boundaries, contradictions, and required abstentions without using real people as training rows.
  6. Fine-tune the private SLM. Teach a compact open model the approved behavior and test it on unseen cases.
  7. Expand authority deliberately. Move from shadow mode to reviewer assistance only after the implementation’s own evidence supports it.

The current work establishes this process on one detailed guide. A second guide is the portability proof.

The engineering principle

Models will change. Company policy will change. Source records will remain messy.

The durable system is the separation of powers: evidence can show without deciding; a guide can define without filling gaps; a model can reason without granting permission; software can constrain without pretending ambiguity is gone; and a person can decide without reconstructing the whole path unaided.
