From Rulebook to Compliance Engine

How evidence, company policy, bounded reasoning, verification, and human review become one auditable decision path.

SNH Strategic InitiativesA Wright OriginalResearch brief · July 2026

The model reasons through the case. The architecture decides whether that reasoning may become an operational decision.

Building a compliance model is not mainly a prompting problem. It is a systems problem: how to preserve the company’s policy, the case evidence, the decision sequence, and the reviewer’s ability to reconstruct what happened after an AI has interpreted the record.

The solution is an architecture with distinct responsibilities. The guide defines authority. The evidence bounds the facts. The SLM handles ambiguity. Deterministic controls enforce exact requirements. A verifier challenges the result. A trace preserves the handoff.

1. Begin with the company’s guide, not a universal answer key

The first design decision is also the most important: the model does not decide what policy should be. It applies the operating guide the company has approved.

That guide may combine law, counsel interpretation, customer requirements, internal risk posture, and review policy. It must be converted into a versioned decision contract that makes precedence and exceptions explicit. When the company changes a rule, the source changes first; testing and model behavior follow.

This is what makes the architecture portable. A different company does not require a completely new software product. It requires a new authorized guide, a new decision contract, and a new validation set.

2. Normalize evidence before asking for judgment

Court and vendor records arrive in inconsistent shapes. The same concept may appear under different field names, in free text, or not at all. Reasoning directly over raw records makes missingness easy to overlook and privacy boundaries hard to enforce.

The engine therefore works from a normalized case packet. It contains only the facts needed for adjudication: jurisdiction, charge, disposition, timing, identity sufficiency, relief, source warnings, and other approved inputs. Missing or contradictory information remains explicit.

This creates one common evidence language for rules, models, evaluators, and reviewers. It also lets the training program use synthetic or masked examples instead of turning real people and historical cases into model-training rows.

3. Apply the guide in procedural order

Compliance guides are sequences, not bags of keywords. Identity may need to be resolved before reportability. Confirmed relief can end the analysis before timing. An offense exclusion can make a later disposition rule irrelevant. A conviction, pending matter, and non-conviction may use different clocks.

The engine preserves that order:

establish identity sufficiency
→ check record relief
→ apply offense exclusions
→ classify disposition and timing
→ decide, or identify the exact reason review is required

Exact branches remain deterministic because they should behave identically every time. The SLM is reserved for the parts that genuinely require interpretation: ambiguous disposition language, interacting facts, exception selection, and explanation.

4. Give the SLM only the evidence it is allowed to use

The SLM with a harness works open-book. It receives the normalized case, the relevant sections of the current company guide, and a fixed output contract. It does not need to search the internet or rely on legal rules remembered from pretraining.

This bounded packet serves two purposes. It reduces distraction, and it makes every final claim testable against the exact material supplied for that case. The model may reason within the packet; it may not silently import a different policy.

5. Treat every model response as an untrusted draft

Fluent reasoning is not sufficient. Before a response becomes an operational decision, the verifier checks whether:

  • the required decision fields are present;
  • the explanation and final outcome agree;
  • every citation belongs to the current guide and the selected case packet;
  • unresolved evidence has not been ignored;
  • the correct policy sequence was followed; and
  • the answer can be connected to the version of the guide used at decision time.

The verifier does not declare independent legal truth. It enforces the company’s decision contract and prevents unsupported model output from acquiring operational authority.

6. Make uncertainty an explicit destination

A background-screening system will encounter missing dates, weak identifiers, unclassifiable dispositions, contradictory source signals, and policy gaps. Forcing every row into a binary answer converts those evidence problems into hidden errors.

The architecture uses manual review as a first-class outcome. When the model cannot support a final decision, the system preserves what is missing, what was attempted, and which rule or evidence boundary stopped the process.

That is useful abstention. It gives a reviewer a narrower question to resolve and gives engineering a measurable failure family to improve.

7. Adjudicate every court case before rolling up the search

One screening search can contain several courts, cases, and charges. A decision based only on the first or most prominent record can miss a reportable sibling case.

The engine evaluates each case separately, preserves its rationale, and then applies a safety-oriented search-level rollup. A disclosable case controls the search. If none is disclosable but one still requires review, review controls. The search is exempt only when every case supports that result.

This structure also makes evaluation more honest: case-level reasoning and search-level operational outcomes can be measured separately rather than collapsed into one ambiguous score.

8. Preserve a trace that connects policy, evidence, and outcome

Every permitted decision carries a reconstruction record: the normalized facts, the guide version, the controlling policy path, the model’s answer, verifier findings, and final disposition.

The trace lets four groups ask different questions without losing context:

  1. Operations can see why a case was decided or routed.
  2. Compliance can see which company rule controlled.
  3. Engineering can isolate whether the failure came from source normalization, policy mapping, reasoning, or verification.
  4. Model training can use approved synthetic traces to teach a fine-tuned SLM without copying historical people or case rows.

9. Turn the governed behavior into a private fine-tuned SLM

Once the guide has been made executable and the governed engine is producing trusted examples, a compact open-source model can learn the company’s adjudication pattern.

The fine-tuned SLM receives normalized facts rather than runtime rule text. Its weights learn the policy sequence, boundary behavior, abstention conditions, and response contract. That makes inference smaller and more portable while preserving a clear benchmark: it must reproduce the approved decision behavior on cases it never saw during training.

The demonstrated 9B-class model is small enough to serve on a single 24 GB workstation or laptop GPU through a standard local model endpoint. The broader system does not care which compatible model supplies the reasoning, so organizations can change weights or hardware without rewriting the policy and verification layers.

10. Keep the two forms distinct

System Runtime input Primary strength Main tradeoff
SLM with a harness Case facts plus selected company rules Current policy, strong provenance, rapid rule changes More runtime components
Fine-tuned SLM Case facts only Private, compact, company-specific inference Must be retrained and re-certified after material policy change

The strongest production design can combine them. The harness remains the source of control and auditability; the fine-tuned SLM becomes the locally hosted reasoner inside it.

What the architecture achieved

The retrospective program processed 24,641 searches containing 82,543 court cases. The SLM with a harness reached 96.1% handbook agreement on its final decisions in the clean model-under-test view, with deterministic decision guards disabled. The strongest fine-tuned SLM reached 94.47% search-level exact agreement and 94.86% case-level accuracy against the decision engine on the full development replay.

Those results matter because the cases were not simple classification prompts. The system had to preserve case boundaries, reason across dispositions and timing, apply exclusions and evidence gates, produce structured decisions, and abstain when the record did not justify certainty.

The architecture was assembled quickly because policy, evidence, reasoning, verification, and training were separate assets. The first working lanes took roughly a week. Once a company guide is mapped and its evaluation contract is stable, new fine-tuned model iterations can be created in hours to days.

What a customer implementation requires

Portability does not eliminate implementation work. Each new company still needs:

  • an authorized operating guide and clear owner for policy decisions;
  • agreement on case facts, review thresholds, and output vocabulary;
  • counsel review of ambiguous or conflicting guide language;
  • a representative held-out evaluation set;
  • privacy and infrastructure controls for the chosen deployment; and
  • a staged operating plan that begins with reviewer assistance.

The reusable architecture makes those decisions explicit. It does not pretend they are the same for every company.

Evidence boundary

The quantitative results come from one retrospective screening program and measure agreement with its encoded operating guide and decision engine. They do not establish universal legal correctness or production autonomy. The 24 GB hardware statement reflects a verified 9B-class local serving configuration; performance, capacity, and infrastructure requirements vary with model size, context, concurrency, and the company’s security controls.
