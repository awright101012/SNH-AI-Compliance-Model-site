One Search, Three Different Decisions

A synthetic case shows how evidence, company policy, an SLM, safeguards, verification, and a reviewer hand control to one another.

SNH Strategic InitiativesA Wright OriginalResearch brief · July 2026

A screening search is not one record and should not receive one unexplained answer.

Consider an illustrative search containing three returned court cases. The facts are synthetic, but the handoffs mirror the operating architecture.

  • Case one contains a disposition the company guide clearly excludes.
  • Case two contains enough evidence for a final reportable decision under the current guide.
  • Case three has an ambiguous disposition and no reliable controlling date.

A search-level classifier is tempted to compress all three into one label. A reviewer can reach the right answer, but only after reconstructing the cases, finding the controlling instructions, and deciding whether the missing evidence matters.

The compliance engine keeps those responsibilities separate.

1. The evidence record shows what arrived

The source adapter creates one bounded case packet for each returned record. It preserves the jurisdiction, charge, disposition, timing, identity evidence, relief indicators, and source warnings needed for adjudication.

Missing data remains visible. Case three does not acquire a date merely because the next step would be easier with one.

The evidence record shows. It does not decide.

2. The company guide defines what is permitted

For each case, the engine selects only the instructions that may control. The guide establishes the order of review and the point at which a case must stop.

In this illustration:

  • the exclusion controls case one;
  • the reportability branch controls case two; and
  • the unresolved disposition and date prevent case three from reaching a final answer.

The guide defines. It does not fill missing evidence.

3. The SLM reasons within that boundary

The SLM receives each case separately with the relevant guide sections. It connects the facts to the instruction, writes a concise explanation, and identifies the controlling policy path.

It does not search for a more convenient rule. It does not combine three records into one invented narrative. It does not get permission to ignore a material warning.

The SLM reasons. It does not grant itself authority.

4. Safeguards remove answers the evidence cannot support

Exact requirements remain software contracts. A case cannot become final if a required identity threshold is not met, a prohibited citation appears, or a material review reason remains unresolved.

For case three, the unresolved evidence prevents both a confident reportable answer and a confident exempt answer. The only permitted destination is review.

Safeguards constrain. They do not interpret genuine ambiguity.

5. The verifier tests the handoff

Before any answer survives, the verifier asks whether the decision, explanation, citations, and guide version reconcile.

Case one must cite the exclusion that actually controlled. Case two must connect the reportable conclusion to the relevant branch. Case three must preserve the unresolved reason instead of disguising it as a completed decision.

An unsupported answer may remain visible as evidence of what the model tried. It may not remain executable as a final compliance decision.

The verifier tests. It does not declare independent legal truth.

6. The search-level decision comes last

Only after every case has an accountable result does the engine roll them into the search outcome.

Case two is reportable, so the search is reportable. Case one remains excluded. Case three remains visible for follow-up review. No sibling case disappears merely because another case controlled the overall search action.

That ordering matters. In the retrospective work, moving from a primary-case shortcut to per-case adjudication eliminated a large apparent conflict set because reportable sibling cases were no longer ignored.

7. A person owns the unresolved judgment

The reviewer does not receive a blank queue item. The record arrives with the separated cases, the provisional decisions, the controlling policy paths, and the exact reason case three could not be finalized.

The system has done the reconstruction work. The person still owns the unresolved judgment.

8. The outcome teaches the next version

If the reviewer resolves the ambiguous disposition, that outcome becomes evaluation evidence. Repeated disagreements can reveal a normalization problem, unclear company guidance, a reasoning failure, or a missing safeguard.

Outcome evidence teaches. It does not automatically rewrite policy or prove why an earlier decision failed.

What changes for the reviewer

Instead of one unexplained search label, the reviewer receives separated cases, the controlling instruction for each, and the exact reason any case remains unresolved. The system does the reconstruction work; the reviewer retains the judgment.
