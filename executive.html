Compliance AI, Built Around Your Rules

Rules engines handle exact branches. Specialists inherit the ambiguity. We built the decision layer between them.

SNH Strategic InitiativesA Wright OriginalResearch brief · July 2026

Executive Summary

  • The missing capability is policy application, not policy storage. Every screening company has an approved way to turn a court record into an action. Rules engines handle exact branches well, but specialists still inherit the records where language, missing evidence, and interacting exceptions must be reconciled.
  • We built two forms of the same decision behavior. An SLM with a harness reasons from the current company guide and leaves a reviewable path. A fine-tuned SLM learns that approved behavior in compact open-source weights that can run inside company-controlled infrastructure.
  • The replay established feasibility at operational scale. Across a large retrospective screening population, both forms demonstrated that compact models can reason through complex criminal cases, preserve structured outputs, and abstain when the evidence does not justify a final decision.
  • The next proof is portability. A shadow-mode implementation using one new company guide and one case family would show whether the method transfers without weakening agreement, abstention, explanation quality, privacy, or reviewer control.

The record is only half the decision

Give the same court record to two screening companies and the facts may be identical while the authorized action differs. Their guides can resolve identity, timing, exclusions, customer requirements, and manual review differently.

The problem is not that specialists lack judgment. It is that the workflow often makes them reconstruct which facts matter, which instruction controls, and where the evidence stops supporting a final answer.

Existing automation remains valuable. It executes exact rules quickly and consistently. It does not interpret an ambiguous disposition, reconcile several returned cases, or decide whether a missing fact is material. When the exact branch ends, a specialist inherits the unresolved reasoning.

A general language model creates a different boundary. It can summarize the record and produce a plausible explanation. It cannot safely guess which company policy it has permission to apply.

The shared missing capability is a decision layer that treats the company guide as authority, lets a small model interpret the record inside that authority, and preserves uncertainty instead of hiding it.

We built the decision layer in two forms

The SLM with a harness works open-book. It receives the normalized case and the relevant guide sections. The walkthrough shows how the answer is checked, routed, and returned to the reviewer.

The fine-tuned SLM works from learned behavior. Synthetic cases teach a compact open-source model the company’s policy sequence, decision boundaries, response contract, and reasons to abstain. That creates a path to private inference without making historical people or case rows into training material.

These are not competing products. The harness is the clearest place to prove and update policy. The fine-tuned SLM is the compact company-specific reasoner that can ultimately operate inside that control architecture.

The handoff is deliberately simple:

Evidence shows. The guide defines. The SLM reasons. Safeguards constrain. The verifier tests. A person decides. Outcomes teach.

The evidence changed three things

Complex adjudication is within reach of compact models

The retrospective replay covered tens of thousands of searches and court cases. The system had to preserve case boundaries, apply exclusions and timing, distinguish several disposition families, produce structured explanations, and abstain when the record did not justify certainty. The evidence figure above carries the exact scope.

This was not a demonstration over a handful of curated prompts. It established that small models can perform the decision work at the scale and messiness of a real screening population.

The same approved behavior can run open-book or from private weights

In the clean model-under-test view, the SLM with a harness agreed with the encoded guide on about 96 of every 100 final decisions it made. Deterministic decision guards were disabled for that measurement, so code-owned answers did not inflate the reasoning result.

The strongest fine-tuned SLM reproduced about 94 of every 100 complete search decisions and about 95 of every 100 individual case decisions without receiving the rulebook at runtime. The exact search-level result appears in the evidence figure; the dedicated fine-tuned SLM article owns the model-specific evaluation detail.

The two measurements answer different questions. The first tests open-book reasoning against the guide. The second tests whether the approved behavior can be learned into compact weights. Neither is independent legal validation, and the fine-tuned development population had already informed improvement. A frozen customer implementation still requires a fresh or sealed holdout.

Private deployment is practical

A 9B-class model was served end to end on one workstation-class GPU through a standard local model endpoint. That does not make every surrounding system private. Storage, logs, networking, and integrations must also remain inside the approved boundary. It does make company-controlled inference achievable without a large model cluster or a per-case call to a general-purpose model provider.

What is proven, what comes next, and what must be earned

Built and tested. One detailed operating guide was converted into a governed decision contract. Both the harnessed and fine-tuned SLMs were exercised across a full retrospective replay. Local 9B-class serving was verified. Model-facing training examples remained synthetic, masked, or normalized.

Next. Convert a second company guide, run recommendations beside human reviewers, and capture complete agreement, disagreement, abstention, and override outcomes. That evidence will establish whether the method transfers and where company policy needs clarification.

Earned future. If a second guide passes its held-out and live-review gates, the company can expand from read-and-recommend to narrow reviewer assistance and, eventually, controlled execution for certified case families. Authority expands one measured role at a time.

What becomes possible next

A shadow-mode implementation with one guide, one record family, reviewer-visible recommendations, and no autonomous authority would create the next evidence the program needs.

The pilot should answer one question:

Can the same architecture learn a second company’s approved decision process while preserving accuracy, abstention, explanation quality, privacy, and reviewer control?

A successful answer would establish something more valuable than another model score. It would establish a repeatable way to build private compliance engines around the way each company actually decides.
